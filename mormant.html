History of Mormant

Mormant is a small town in north-central France. The town is located in the Seine-et-Marne department in the Île-de-France region. The town of Mormant belongs to the arrondissement of Melun and the canton of Mormant. The postal code of the city of Mormant is 77720 and its INSEE code is 77317. The inhabitants of Mormant are called Mormantais and Mormantaises.
Shortly before Mormant, Victor met a Russian corps of 8,000 men, who refused the fight, withdrew and entrenched themselves in the village of Mormant. Seeing the enemy entrenched, General Gérard rushed to the attack at the head of the 5th Battalion of the 32nd Line Regiment. In his momentum, he threw the Russians out of the village and captured two enemy battalions. The Russian general Peter von der Palhen evacuated the village, while his cavalry tried to cover the retreating vanguard. Suddenly, general Piré's light cavalry and General Trelliard's dragoons, which had bypassed the Russo-Bavarian positions, flanked the Russian hunters and lancers and threw them down. Continuing their momentum, the French cavalry caught up with the coalition infantry which had just come under heavy fire from a battery of 36 pieces of artillery of the Guard commanded by Drouot. Desperately, the Russo-Bavarians are forming as well as they can in two squares. One, assailed by kellermann and milhaudriders,is sunk and surrenders. The other tries to escape to the marshes ofAncoeur,but is annihilated by the brigades Ismert (4th,10th dragoons), and Ludot (13th and 16th dragoons). .
